Introduction

This project was developed within the framework of the KE4H (Knowledge Engineering for the Humanities) course at the University of Bologna . This page aims to explore the history and architecture of Santa Maria del Fiore by combining traditional historical research with digital tools such as SPARQL queries and Large Language Models (LLMs). These tools are used to retrieve, analyze, and enrich information about the cathedral within cultural heritage knowledge graphs.

Analizing Santa Maria del Fiore, using knowledge graphs is important because it allows information about the cathedral—such as its history, architects, artworks, and structural features to be represented in a structured and interconnected way. This makes it easier to link data from different sources, discover hidden relationships, and enrich existing cultural heritage information using digital tools like SPARQL queries and Large Language Models.

As our base knowledge graph, we selected ArCo – the official ontology network and RDF dataset of the Italian Ministry of Culture. ArCo offers a rich and well-structured representation of Italy’s cultural heritage and provides an excellent SPARQL endpoint for advanced semantic exploration. Its modular architecture and domain-specific ontologies made it a strong choice for our project’s objectives, especially in the context of tangible heritage.
