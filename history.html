History

The history of the Cattedrale di Santa Maria del Fiore is a monumental saga of human ambition and engineering genius that spans several centuries. Construction began in 1296 on the site of the ancient church of Santa Reparata, based on a design by Arnolfo di Cambio, intended to manifest the growing wealth and prestige of Florence. The project faced significant delays and structural challenges, most notably the "missing" dome, which remained an open-air puzzle for over a century due to its unprecedented scale and lack of external buttressing. It was not until 1420 that Filippo Brunelleschi—an unlikely visionary with a background in goldsmithing—began work on his self-supporting, double-shelled brick dome. Completed in 1436, the dome stood as a crowning achievement of the early Renaissance and remains the largest masonry dome in the world. While the main structure was finished by the 15th century, the cathedral's iconic multi-colored marble façade was not completed until the late 19th century (1871–1887), finally realizing a grand design that has served as the heart of Florence’s spiritual and civic identity for over 700 years.
