History

Its history began in 1296 on the site of the older cathedral of Santa Reparata under the direction of Arnolfo di Cambio, aiming to create the largest church in Tuscany to reflect the growing power and wealth of the Florentine Republic. Over the following century, the project saw contributions from iconic figures such as Giotto, who designed the adjacent campanile, and Andrea Pisano, though progress was frequently slowed by the sheer ambition of the design and the limitations of medieval engineering. The most critical turning point occurred in 1418, when a public competition was held to solve the seemingly impossible task of roofing the enormous central crossing, a challenge won by Brunelleschi. The completion of his dome in 1436 marked a pivotal moment in the transition from the Middle Ages to the Renaissance, and although the building remained largely complete for centuries, the facade underwent its final transformation in the late 19th century when Emilio de Fabris added the intricate Neo-Gothic design seen today.
